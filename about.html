About

I mostly write about trading, stats, and whatever math trick I’m experimenting with at the moment. Think of this space as a mix of quant notes and random dev musings I have explored in quant finance.

I didn’t exactly plan to end up in quant space until high school. At IIT Roorkee I was more into economics and applied math—speed, efficiency, and problem-solving. Markets came later, almost by accident. Once I started digging into option pricing, volatility models, and systematic trading, it clicked: the marketplace was the perfect sandbox to bring all the pieces together.

For me, algotrading isn’t just about finding an “edge.” It’s about blending math, market knowledge, and code into something elegant that actually runs. It’s equal parts theory, craft, and curiosity—and that balance is what keeps me hooked.
