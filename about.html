About

These are my notes and musings on trading, statistics, and whatever math idea I’ve been playing with lately. It’s less of a polished blog and more of a place where I park things I find interesting in quant finance.

I didn’t start out aiming for the quant world. In my first year at IIT Roorkee, I was more drawn to economics — curious about how markets worked on a big-picture level. Somewhere along the way I wandered into quantitative finance, and it stuck. The mix of math, code, and markets felt more real than theory — it was something you could test, break, and rebuild.

These days, I mostly explore strategies, backtests, and numerical experiments that catch my attention. This space is just a way to document what I learn, and maybe make sense of the noise as I go.

If something here resonates or sparks an idea, feel free to reach out.
